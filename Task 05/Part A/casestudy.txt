Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common types of cybercrime in India. In this type of fraud, attackers trick users into sharing sensitive details or approving fake payment requests. A typical scenario involves a fraudster posing as a bank official or a buyer/seller on an online platform.

For example, a student selling a product online may receive a message from a buyer who claims to send payment through UPI. Instead of sending money, the fraudster sends a "collect request" and asks the victim to enter their UPI PIN to receive the payment. If the victim enters the PIN, money gets deducted from their account instead of being credited.

These scams usually target students and individuals who are not fully aware of how digital payments work. The consequences can include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online platforms.

This case highlights the importance of understanding how UPI systems work and being cautious while accepting payment requests from unknown sources.
